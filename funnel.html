Top of Funnel · Attract

Stop the scroll.
Get on the radar.

Who it is for

Dentists and office managers who do not know Adit and are not thinking about their missed-call problem. The job here is not to sell. It is to be relatable, useful, and impossible to scroll past, so Adit earns a place in their feed.

Post styles that live here
  • Relatable pain reels: the front desk drowning, the phone that never stops ringing
  • Humor that gets shared: the wrong-tool face, the chaos every office knows
  • Scroll-stopping hooks: "the most expensive phone call is the one nobody answers"
  • Rapid-fire team content for reach and discovery
For example

"Who's really on the other end?" Six missed calls, six lost patients, named one by one. Ends with a soft comment prompt, not a pitch.

Soft CTA: follow, save, or comment a keyword for the breakdown. No hard sell at this stage.

Middle of Funnel · Nurture

Build trust.
Prove the solution.

Who it is for

Problem-aware followers and commenters weighing their options. They feel the pain now. The job is to educate on the fix, differentiate Adit, and answer the objection before they raise it.

Post styles that live here
  • "Listen to a real AI call": the product doing the job, live
  • "What Adit actually is": connects to your software, not a rip and replace
  • Objection reels: it expands your front desk, it does not replace your team
  • Client story teasers: the before, the turning point
For example

"AI won't replace your front desk. It expands it." Answers the number-one fear head on, then shows the same call handled two ways.

Tracked CTA: comment the keyword, ManyChat sends the resource and the booking link. Intent captured, demo one tap away.

Bottom of Funnel · Close

Turn intent
into a booked demo.

Who it is for

Warm leads, engaged followers, and contacts met at events who are ready to move. The job is to remove the last bit of doubt and make booking the obvious next step.

Post styles that live here
  • Case studies with real numbers: production added, new patients, booking rate
  • "What a missed call costs" calculator: book a demo to see your own number
  • Direct book-a-demo CTAs and retargeting to warm viewers
  • Event conversion: come book a live demo at booth, thanks for stopping by
For example

"This practice added five figures in production." A client case study with the numbers, closing on a direct booking link.

Tracked CTA: the booking link is traceable, the demo lands in Zoho attributed to the platform and vertical it came from.

The Flywheel · Refer

4,000 clients,
turned into growth.

Who it is for

The 4,000 practices already paying Adit, the biggest warm-referral pool in dental, sitting untouched. The job is to feature them, let them share, and turn their wins into inbound demos and warm introductions.

Post styles that live here
  • Client feature reels: a happy practice, made to look great, primed to share
  • Testimonials and UGC from the existing base
  • "Why I switched" client-voice stories that convince peers
  • Warm intros Felipe converts with a sales background
For example

A featured client shares their reel. Their peers in the same groups see a doctor like them winning with Adit, which feeds new awareness and warm intros back into the top of the funnel.

The loop: proof at the bottom becomes attraction at the top. The funnel feeds itself.

The Spine

Every stage runs
on one tracked path.

Whatever the stage, the mechanism is the same and it is always measurable. Tailored to the group, posted on Adit's accounts through Metricool, captured by ManyChat, closed into Zoho.

Tailored to the group → Posted via Metricool → One-word comment → ManyChat auto-DM → Booking link → Tracked demo in Zoho

Followers that never convert do not count. Every booked demo is attributed by platform and vertical, so we can prove which stage and which post produced it. The strategy is not the reels. It is this: the right message, in the right room, with a tracked next step.
